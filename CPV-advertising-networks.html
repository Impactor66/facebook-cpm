Campaign Targeting


There are various effective methods of campaign targeting. Layering multiple targeting methods can overly restrict the reach of the advertiser's ad group. The advertiser must make sure to monitor his ad's performance to check that he is seeing the results he wants.


Campaign Targeting By Device Specifications:

Cell audiences are evolving continuously and the majority of ad budgets are now spent on carefully targeted rather than wide-reaching campaigns intended for anyone. It is now extremely important to ensure appropriate ad content reaches appropriate users and that your advert is optimized for the specifications of the apparatus utilized by the visitor. Device type might be an important filter for campaign optimization by means of segregating hardware types such as tablets, notebooks, smartphones and personal computers. Device detection is an essential ingredient to make this possible. It’s also a technical issue due to different screen sizes, screen resolutions, hardware capabilities, and connectivity levels. It’s important to know what type of device is being served, so it can be served correctly. Page weight and loading speed ought to be optimized via device detection. This type of targeting method opens the door to target specific device manufacturers, device-types and -models as well as specific screen resolutions and operating systems. You can combine multiple device specifications to narrow your target.


  1. Device Type, Device Model

    - including but not limited to Internet of Things, smart TVs, cameras, game consoles, etc.. Detecting the device type is done via cloud detection and/or local detection or a hybrid of both

  2. Device Make and Brand

    - e.g. Apple iPhone X. Having the ability to target specific device models in your arsenal is very cutting edge, especially when it comes to campaign optimization. It allows you to exclude certain devices that have a high traffic volume but that traffic does not convert well on those devices or the other way around, then you include / whitelist specific device models.

  3. Device Year of Release

    - When analysing campaign reports and its data specific device years might stand out and therefore those devices should be focused on.

  4. Device Capabilities

    - Near Field Communication, Long Term Evolution, band, etc.

Environment Targeting:

  1. Operating systems

    - Targeting by operating system means you may target users who are using specific operating systems. When promoting a product or a service that is relevant only for a particular operating system, then this targeting feature is crucial. While promoting a mobile app on Android (APK) for example you certainly don’t want any iOS devices in your traffic. Operating system targeting allows the advertiser to include or exclude certain operating systems, or even entire operating system families but also specific OS-versions.

  2. Browsers

    - At first glance this might not make any sense. While running a campaign however tracking might detect that one browser performs better than another or a certain browser version uses some kind of ad-blocker which makes driving traffic to that browser type obsolete.

  3. Screen Resolutions and Screen Size

    - With emerging device fragmentation and hundreds of different screen resolutions - including but not limited to diagonal screen sizes, from a 4-inch phone to a massive 5.7-inch phablet - categorizing and being able to target specific screen resolutions can be an important factor in campaign optimization. There are now over 1,000 device manufacturers producing phones and tablets.

  4. Mobile Carrier Targeting

    - There are multiple mechanism of an ad network do mobile carrier identification. The challenge of all those methods is to detect origin ip and then lookup which carrier would the ip belong to. The ad network might and should have a license for ip to carrier database lookup from providers like maxmind. Or the ad network has built its own over time. The accuracy of this detection depends on the quality and accuracy of the data provided by the data source. The most accurate way has proven to be if the Mobile Ad network can get the MCC (Mobile Country Code) and MNC (Mobile Network Code). This however only works for applications. In cases where the MNC and MCC is not detectable you could use an IP database like IP2Location to detect the carrier. But don’t count on 100% accuracy.

  5. Wireless Networks

    - Targeting wireless networks as a targeting option is targeting all traffic from wireless networks to the exclusion of all other networks.

  6. Connection Targeting

    - With the connection speeds targeting feature provided by many ad networks you can determine if you want to target Wifi-, cellular Carrier-Traffic or any other of all the remaining connection types. While Long Term Evolution (4G mobile communications standard) coverage is increasing in every geo, mobile phone users still suffer from a lot of connection speed problems. Bandwidth issues are not just slow connectivity but a limited amount of data that can be downloaded during a given period. Once the data volume allowance is depleted, users deal with data throttling by intentional slowing of internet connection by the mobile carrier and making charges per MB more expensive.

    1. Connection type (Business, Internet Cafe, Cellular, College, Government, Library, Military, Residential, School, Traveler)

    2. Connection speeds: dial-up, cable / Digital Subscriber Line Broadband, Cellular / Carrier


Campaign Targeting By Categories, Niche, Interests, Demographics, Geos and Budget:

  1. Budgets

    - Generally mobile traffic tends to be way cheaper than desktop traffic. Your ad spend also decides about the quality of traffic you get from the ad network.

  2. Categories

    - More and more ad networks attribute their publishers' websites to the standard IAB categories depending on their content type. Such information is then displayed to potential advertisers looking for audience targeting within a specific category and the advertiser with the highest bid request gets the placement on a specific site.

  3. Niche Targeting

    - Niche advertising is a targeted plan that specializes in one specific segment of the marketplace that has excessive ability to hook up with a service or product. Instead of casting a wide net in mass-media and big-event advertising, niche marketing banks on strategically filtered traffic sources and media platforms which have excessive concentrations of this target audience. There are three campaign types where niche targeting is reflected:

    1. Word-of-Mouth Referral-Campaigns

    2. Endorsement Campaigns

    3. Targeted Collateral Campaigns

  4. Interests

    - Targeting by Interest is by far the easiest to implement and best converting targeting method, which is perfect for any kind of content marketing and contextual advertising. It achieves higher click through rates thanks to the matching congruency between publisher’s content and advertiser’s campaign interests.

  5. Demographics

    - age, gender, income, marital status

  6. Geo Targeting

    - there are the following two methods:

    1. Geo Targeting by IP Intelligence

      This is the most essential option to attract new customers to a specific point of sale.

      1. Geotargeting implies the features of targeting specific streets, districts, cities, municipalities, regions, states, provinces, territories, countries, airport data, autonomous communities, cantons, congressional, counties, departments, designated market areas, governorates, Okrug administrative divisions, zip codes, postal codes, prefecture, television region data and union territories.
      2. Hyper-Geotargeting (that goes hand in hand with hyper-geolocalisation) is a kind of geotargeting that allows for targeting of a specific point with a specific address radius up to 100 m.
      3. Geo-Fencing which is a virtual perimeter for a real-world predefined geographic area. Location based targeting options depend on the targeting options provided by the ad network.

    2. Geo Targeting by Language

      - Geo-targeting via language is based on the assumption that the device user speaks the same language as the language set in the user’s browser. It is like a guarantee that the user understand the ad.


Society Targeting:

  1. Language

    - Audience targeting by Language allows the advertiser to select the language of the sites and their placement options that he'd like his ads to appear on. Language targeting lets the marketer reach an audience that speaks one or multiple languages, allowing him to reach his target audience even if it is physically located in non-native areas. Language targeting offers great advantages to advertisers who want to reach a specific segment of the population or promote their products and services in countries, metropolitan areas and cities where large populations speak multiple languages.

  2. Populations of a Certain Size

  3. Unique User

    - Frequency capping option utilizes user ID to ensure a proper number of ads are shown to the exact same user within the campaign. User IDs are also transferred in the bid request and could be used for retargeting.

  4. Behavior

    - such as user’s browser history, purchase history, events the user likes, personal anniversaries and other recent activity. Depending on the ad network this data is being gathered internally, just like Facebook does, or externally by by using external data sets and data scraping.

This optimization method helps to decrease the number of non-effective impressions and increase the overall campaign ROI.

Optimizing for viewability and matching target-audience significantly enhances conversion impact for advertisers, alignment with media sellers is crucial to meeting campaign goals and in-target reporting improves precisions of reported campaign performance data

Retargeting

Retargeting is an advertising method with over 250 billion impressions per day throughout 195 countries. Retargeting tracks and observes online activity of 750 million people worldwide. With second generation retargeting the advertiser follows up with a lead without having any of their contact information and without having the lead visit the advertiser’s website. The audience available for retargeting consists of 203,232,000 “in the market” prospects in 10,750 categories and niches.


  1. First Generation Retargeting:

    Here is how it works:

    • User visits website;

    • User gets tracking cookie;

    • User continually sees advertiser's ad while navigating the web.

    Pros:

  • It offers the highest ROI advertising a business can do online.

  • Retargeting Allows The Advertiser

    1. to stay top of mind with the consumer by following him around on the internet
    2. to display impressions on sites with lower costs, but the advertiser is still hitting the same person - more effective cost per impression. Repetition is extremely important in advertising.

    Cons:

    1. Still too expensive - in comparison with second generation retargeting

    2. Not targeted (enough)

    3. Cost per click based


    1. Second Generation Retargeting:

      Pros:

    • Building the retargeting list based on ad ad impressions, rather than clicks.

    • Impression based retargeting: Every time the marketer’s ad is shown, someone is added to his list. With $3 CPM a thousand people are being added to the marketer’s list.

    • The lead does not have to click the advert and does not have to visit the website of the advertising business. But the lead is on the advertiser’s retargeting list.

    • Keyword level retargeting: Target and build a retargeting list based on keywords

    • Target and build a retargeting list based on specific pages and URLs

    • Build a retargeting list based on impressions and NOT clicks.

    • Capabilities of Second Generation Retargeting:

      • Real time bidding,

      • International Geo targeting,

      • Online activity of 750 million people worldwide is being tracked and observed,

      • Over 15 million keywords tracked.

      • Lead capacity in finance: 10,808,000 “in the market” uniques per month

      • Lead capacity in Retail: 144,063,000 “in the market” uniques per month

      • Lead capacity in Auto: 27,698,000 “in the market” uniques per month

        - “In the market” means people who are currently searching or visiting certain pages about certain topics the marketer has previously defined in his campaign. They are actively seeking whatever those topics are about.

      • 296 ad networks, ad exchanges, and traffic aggregators, 40 million websites, completely owning the traffic, clickstream data access

      Cons:

        Relatively new and still too unknown.
